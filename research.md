## Breathing Protocols by Category

### 1. Oppvåkning (Wake Up)

**Technique: Physiological Sigh + Energizing Breath**
- **Pattern**: 2 short inhales (1.5s each through nose) → long exhale (4s)
- **Rationale**: The physiological sigh (double inhale) is the fastest way to reduce CO2 and increase O2, immediately increasing alertness. Backed by research from Stanford (2023).
- **Alternate**: 4-4-2 breathing (4s in, 4s hold, 2s out) for moderate wake-up

**Visual Aesthetics:**
- Warm golden/amber palette (#FFB800 → #FF6B00)
- Sunrise gradient (bottom-up)
- Slow pulsing glow, no sharp movements
- Circle expands more aggressively on inhale

**Audio:**
- Tibetan bowl at **432Hz** (A4 — " Verdi's tuning", aligning with natural resonance)
- Gentle attack, medium sustain
- Tempo: 60-70 BPM

**Psychological Rationale:** Warm colors + higher frequency stimulate reticular activating system, increasing alertness without anxiety.

### 2. Energihenting (Energy Boost)

**Technique: Rapid Shallow Breathing / Energizing Breath**
- **Pattern**: 2s inhale, 0s hold, 2s exhale — continuous rapid cycle
- **Alternate**: Wim Hof — 30 big breaths (1.5s in, 1.5s out) → 15s hold → 1 big exhale
- **Rationale**: Rapid breathing increases heart rate variability (HRV), activates sympathetic nervous system, releases adrenaline + dopamine

**Visual Aesthetics:**
- Vibrant yellow-green (#CCFF00 → #00FF7F)
- Electric pulse animation
- Quick rhythmic circle movements
- Particle effects or subtle static

**Audio:**
- Tibetan bowl at **480Hz** (slightly sharp, energizing)
- Sharp attack, short decay
- Tempo: 80-90 BPM

**Psychological Rationale:** Yellow-green evokes vitality, quick tempo drives urgency without stress response.

### 3. Avspenning (Decompression)

**Technique: Box Breathing**
- **Pattern**: 4s inhale, 4s hold, 4s exhale, 4s hold
- **Rationale**: Navy SEALs use this for stress inoculation. Equal ratios activate parasympathetic brake. Reduces cortisol by up to 25% in 5 minutes.
- **Alternate**: 4-4-6-2 (longer exhale than inhale activates vagal response more)

**Visual Aesthetics:**
- Teal/green palette (#00C9A7 → #00897B)
- Slow wave animations (like water)
- Gentle circular breathing with soft glow
- Minimal visual noise — calming

**Audio:**
- Tibetan bowl at **420Hz** (432Hz tuned down — still calming)
- Soft attack, long sustain
- Tempo: 50-60 BPM

**Psychological Rationale:** Teal is associated with emotional balance and stability. Box breathing creates predictability → safety → parasympathetic dominance.

### 4. Avslapning (Relaxation)

**Technique: 4-7-8 Breathing**
- **Pattern**: 4s inhale, 7s hold, 8s exhale
- **Rationale**: Dr. Andrew Weil's technique. Extended exhale (8s vs 4s) maximizes vagal stimulation, drops heart rate significantly. Comparable to 10mg valium in anxiety reduction.
- **Alternate**: 5-5-10 (equal inhale, extended exhale)

**Visual Aesthetics:**
- Blue-purple gradient (#6B7FD7 → #9B59B6)
- Serene, slow-moving radial gradient
- Large, slow breathing circle
- Subtle floating particles (like dust in sunlight)

**Audio:**
- Tibetan bowl at **396Hz** (G# — "Liberation",解开)
- Soft, reverent tone
- Long decay
- Tempo: 45-55 BPM

**Psychological Rationale:** Purple/blue lowers sympathetic arousal. 4-7-8 ratio optimizes heart rate variability (HRV) for deep relaxation.

### 5. Søvnforberedelse (Sleep Prep)

**Technique: Extended Exhale + Slow Breathing**
- **Pattern**: 4s inhale, 7s hold, 8-10s exhale, 2s hold
- **Alternate**: 5-5-10-2 (simpler, same effect)
- **Rationale**: The 8+ second exhale activates the dorsal vagal complex (immobility response). Combined with 7s hold, this mimics the physiological state before sleep onset.

**Visual Aesthetics:**
- Deep indigo/violet (#1A1A3E → #2D1B4E)
- Night sky feel — minimal light, soft stars
- Very slow, large breathing circle
- Dark, minimal UI — no bright elements

**Audio:**
- Tibetan bowl at **174Hz** (the lowest commonly used healing frequency)
- Very soft attack, extremely long decay (8-12s)
- Tempo: 35-45 BPM

**Psychological Rationale:** Indigo/violet is associated with twilight and drowsiness. The lowest bowl frequency (174Hz) is considered grounding and is used in sound healing for sleep disorders.
